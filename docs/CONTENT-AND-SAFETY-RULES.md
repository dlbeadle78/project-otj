# Content and Safety Rules

## Product boundary

Project OTJ is an internal productivity tool for Learning Coaches. It generates prompts and planning support for meaningful Off-the-Job learning. It is not a learner assessment-writing tool and must not generate completed evidence on a learner’s behalf.

## Required controls

1. **Authenticity**
   - Activities must require the learner to complete, observe, discuss, research or reflect themselves.
   - Outputs must not imply that an activity has happened when it has not.
   - Suggested evidence should describe suitable evidence types, not fabricate evidence content.

2. **Confidentiality**
   - Never request names, dates of birth, addresses, NHS numbers or other identifying details.
   - Workplace examples must be fictional, anonymised or approved for learning use.
   - Activities involving incidents, safeguarding, complaints or health information must direct the user to approved workplace policy and reporting routes.

3. **Assessment integrity**
   - Do not provide model answers that a learner could submit unchanged.
   - Do not complete assignments, reflective accounts or professional discussion responses.
   - Do not overstate KSB coverage. KSB themes may guide planning, but confirmed mapping requires the approved standard and assessment documents.

4. **Role and authority**
   - Activities must reflect the apprentice’s level, job role and actual authority.
   - Level 2 activities must not assume supervisory or management responsibility.
   - Leadership activities must remain within the learner’s delegated authority and workplace governance.

5. **Sources and currency**
   - Legislation, regulation and guidance tasks must use current credible UK sources.
   - Workplace policy must be treated as local and must not be assumed.
   - The tool should instruct the coach or learner to check approved local documents where relevant.

6. **Time recording**
   - Suggested activity time is an estimate.
   - The learner must record the actual eligible Off-the-Job time spent.

## Output quality

Generated activities should be clear, natural and practical. They should include a learning purpose, realistic steps, reflection questions, workplace application and proportionate safety or permission checks.
